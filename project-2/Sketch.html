Cities & Memory

These cities are assembled from recollection: plazas shaped like forgotten afternoons, bridges echoing footsteps of those long gone. To walk here is to wander through your own past, rearranged into new geometries. Each corner feels familiar, yet no map you carry matches what you see.

Cities & Desire

Desire stretches the streets and pulls towers upward, filling the air with the scent of what has not yet happened. These cities shimmer with possibilities: each doorway a promise, each window a longing. The plan redraws itself around what you seek, as if the grid were following your gaze.

Cities & Signs

Here, nothing is what it seems. A fountain may signify departure; a staircase may mean return. Travelers learn to read the city as one reads a text, deciphering symbols left by those who passed before. The blueprint is dense with annotations, but none of them agree on what the city truly is.

Thin Cities

Suspended between earth and sky, these cities balance on threads, ropes, and breath. Their structures are so light that a whispered story can shift the entire skyline. On the plan they appear as faint lines and dotted spans, as if the architect were unsure whether to commit them to paper at all.

Trading Cities

Goods move through these cities, but so do stories. Every exchange alters the city’s shape: a market stall becomes a memory, a negotiation becomes a new street. The blueprint shows arrows, loops, and crossings, yet the true traffic is in the tales that change hands at every intersection.

A digital blueprint atlas inspired by Italo Calvino’s Invisible Cities.
