One ritual that has been central to my psychological wellbeing is calling my family once every week. It’s a practice I started when I first left home for boarding school in middle school and one that still grounds me today. The conversations are often unremarkable, just small updates about daily life, but the act itself reminds me that I belong somewhere and that there are people whose lives are woven into mine regardless of distance. In moments when life feels overwhelming, that quiet consistency is enough to center me back. I think this reveals how human fulfilment for me at least doesn’t require any grand gestures or commitment, rather a small, repeated acts of connection and consistency.
